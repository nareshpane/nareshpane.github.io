Research

This page gathers some of my current computational and mathematical research interests. The projects below reflect my interest in graph theory, experimentation, and careful quantitative analysis.

  • Testing the Limits of the nauty Package for Checking Ramsey Number (5,5)
    Computational experiments on graph generation, pruning strategies, non-isomorphic reduction, and feasibility questions arising in the search around the lower bound for \(R(5,5)\).
  • Empirically Validating Evolution of Giant Components in Random Graphs
    A computational study of the emergence and growth of giant components in random graphs, with attention to simulation, repeated sampling, and comparison with theoretical limiting behavior.
