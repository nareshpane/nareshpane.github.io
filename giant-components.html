Empirically Validating Evolution of Giant Components in Random Graphs

This project examines the emergence and development of giant components in random graphs through repeated simulation and computational observation.

I am interested in how empirical behavior compares with the classical theoretical picture, especially when one records graph evolution step by step, averages across many runs, and studies the onset of large connected structures.

More broadly, this work reflects my interest in combining probability, graph theory, and statistical computing to test limiting phenomena through carefully designed experiments.
