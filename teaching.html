Teaching

The courses below reflect my teaching assistant work at the University of Victoria while I was pursuing my Master of Arts in Economics from September 2018 to August 2020 and, later, my Master of Science in Statistics from September 2022 to December 2024.

My teaching-related work spanned economics and statistics, including econometrics, sampling, and linear algebra. The table below records the courses, the terms in which I assisted, the professors for whom I worked, and brief course descriptions based on official UVic materials.

Official UVic undergraduate calendar and related course information: University of Victoria Undergraduate Calendar

Courses Assisted

Course Title University Term Professor Course Description
MATH 211 – Matrix Algebra I
 University of Victoria Fall 2024 Dr. William Thompson Introduces core topics in matrix algebra, including matrices and simultaneous equations, determinants, vectors, inner products, linear independence, rank, coordinate changes, orthogonal matrices, and eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
STAT 354 – Sampling Techniques
 University of Victoria Spring 2023 Prof. Julie Zhou Focuses on the design and implementation of sample surveys, including how to plan and conduct a sample survey in a statistically sound way.
ECON 345 – Applied Econometrics I
 University of Victoria Fall 2019 Prof. Felix Pretis Covers regression analysis, including the properties of OLS estimators and test statistics under alternative model assumptions. Additional topics may include functional form, binary variables, F-tests of linear restrictions, heteroskedasticity, instrumental variables estimation, and implications of estimating regression models with time-series data.
ECON 345 – Applied Econometrics I
 University of Victoria Spring 2019 Prof. Alan Mehlenbacher Introduces applied econometric analysis with an emphasis on regression methods, practical empirical work, and the interpretation of econometric results in economic applications.
Mathematical Models in Economics I
 University of Victoria Fall 2018; Summer 2019 Prof. Alan Mehlenbacher A mathematically oriented economics course emphasizing the analytical tools used in economics, including calculus-based reasoning, optimization, and model-based thinking. The underlying focus is on using mathematical structure to formulate and analyze economic problems.
