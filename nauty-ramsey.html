Testing the Limits of the nauty Package for Checking Ramsey Number (5,5)

This project studies the computational feasibility of using the nauty package and related graph-generation workflows to explore candidates relevant to the Ramsey number \(R(5,5)\).

My interest here is not only in brute-force generation, but in understanding how far one can push practical computation through structural pruning, edge and degree restrictions, non-isomorphic reduction, and post-generation testing.

The broader goal is to examine where computational bottlenecks arise, what kinds of screening criteria are most useful, and how graph-theoretic insight can reduce otherwise prohibitive search spaces.
